### Based on YTC Price Action Trader by Lance Beggs

## The Core Mental Model

> **Markets are not random — they are the aggregate behavior of humans acting on fear and greed, uncertainty and hope, at every price level.**

Your edge does not come from predicting the future. It comes from **identifying high-probability situations where the crowd's emotional behavior is likely to repeat**, and positioning yourself to profit from that repetition.

**Scientific basis:** behavioral finance + market microstructure + probability theory.

## Foundation 1 — Principles of Markets
> *YTC Vol 2, Chapter 2*

### Theory to Master

| Concept | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Price = consensus of value at this moment | Nothing more, nothing less |
| Price moves because consensus shifts | Buyers overwhelm sellers or vice versa |
| Candle wicks (shadows) tell the truth | They show rejection — emotion made visible |
| Cause & Effect | Every move has a reason; find the cause |
| Markets alternate: trend ↔ consolidation | Never in one state forever |

### The Probability Frame

You are not trying to be right. You are trying to find situations where the **expected value (EV) is positive** across many trades.

- One loss means nothing
- 100 trades mean everything

### Human Emotion Layer

| Emotion | How It Manifests in Price |
|---|---|
| Fear of missing out (FOMO) | Creates breakout traps |
| Fear of loss | Causes premature exits |
| Greed | Causes overtrading and holding losers |
| Hope | Replaces analysis — the most dangerous state |

## Foundation 2 — Market Analysis
> *YTC Vol 2, Chapter 3*

This is your **situational awareness** layer. Answer these before every trade.

### Step 1 — Initial Market Analysis (before session opens)

```
1. Where are the key Support & Resistance levels?  (price memory = crowd memory)
2. What is the higher timeframe trend?
3. What is the lower timeframe trend?
4. Where are the areas of strength vs weakness?
5. What is the most likely next move?
```

### Step 2 — Ongoing Market Analysis (bar by bar, during session)

```
1. What is the candle pattern telling me about sentiment?
2. Does this action confirm or contradict my bias?
3. Does a setup area exist here?
```

### Key Concepts

**Multiple Timeframe Analysis**
Higher TF sets the context, lower TF provides the entry. Never fight the higher timeframe.

**Market Structure**
Swing highs/lows form the skeleton of the market. Learn to read them before anything else.

**Strength & Weakness**
Strong moves leave little pullback. Weak moves get deeply retraced. This is the footprint of conviction.

**Support & Resistance**
Not magic lines. They are price levels where humans previously made decisions and remember doing so. Price returns because they act again.

## Foundation 3 — Trading Strategy & Setups
> *YTC Vol 3, Chapters 4–5*

### The Expectancy Formula — Your Probability Anchor

```
Expectancy = (Win% × Avg Win) - (Loss% × Avg Loss)
```

> A system with 40% wins can be MORE profitable than one with 70% wins
> — if the average win is 2.5× the average loss.

**Risk:reward ratio is more important than win rate.**

---

### What Is a Setup?

A **setup** is a specific market condition where the probability of price moving in your favour is higher than random — justified by a reason, not a feeling.

It has three components that must all be present:

```
1. LOCATION  — where price is (a meaningful S/R level, structure area)
2. SIGNAL    — what price is doing at that location (rejection, confirmation)
3. CONTEXT   — does the higher timeframe agree with the trade direction?
```

If any one of these is missing, it is not a setup — it is a guess.

**The three questions a setup must answer with "yes":**

| Question | What You're Checking |
|---|---|
| Is price at a meaningful area? | Location has value — not random space |
| Is there evidence of the move starting? | Price is showing its hand |
| Is the broader market aligned? | You're not fighting the trend |

All three yes = setup exists. Enter with a defined stop and target.
Any one no = stand aside and wait.

**The correct order — most beginners get this wrong:**

```
1. Identify the location first  (where should I be watching?)
2. Wait for price to arrive     (patience)
3. Watch for the signal         (is it showing intent to move?)
4. Check context                (higher TF agrees?)
5. Then and only then — execute
```

> A setup is not something you find. It is something you **wait for**.

---

### The 5 Setup Types

All are variations of one idea: **price arrives at a significant area and shows evidence of holding or breaking.**

| Setup | Description | Emotional Driver |
|---|---|---|
| **TST** — Test of Support/Resistance | Price probes a level, shows rejection | Trapped traders panic, fuel the reversal |
| **BOF** — Breakout Failure | Break fails and reverses | Breakout traders trapped, they fuel the move back |
| **BPB** — Break-Pull-Back | Confirmed break, then pullback to the broken level | Latecomers join, early traders add to position |
| **PB** — Pullback in Trend | Trend continuation after a retracement | Dominant emotional bias; pullback = opportunity |
| **CPB** — Complex Pullback | Multi-leg pullback before continuation | Tests weak hands more thoroughly than simple PB |

---

### Entry / Stop / Target Structure

```
STOP
└── Placed where your setup thesis is proven wrong
    (just beyond the structure that defines the setup)

TARGET 1 (T1)
└── First logical obstacle — nearest S/R
    Exit half position here to lock in profit

TARGET 2 (T2)
└── If market structure allows, trail the remainder
    Let winners run while protecting capital

ENTRY
└── On confirmation — not prediction
    Wait for evidence the setup is triggering
    Enter on the retest, not the initial move
```

## Foundation 4 — Your Trading Business
> *YTC Vol 4*

Trading full-time is a **business**, not a hobby with larger size. Most part-time traders fail this transition because they skip this layer.

### Business Structure Required

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Trading Plan | Written, specific, non-negotiable rules |
| Procedures Manual | Step-by-step process for every session |
| Money Management Rules | Fixed % risk per trade (1–2% max) |
| Contingency Management | What to do during system failures and drawdowns |
| Goals & Stats Tracking | Track expectancy, not just P&L |

### Money Management Reality

- Risk **1–2% of account per trade** maximum
- Define a **daily loss limit** (e.g., 3 losses = session over, no exceptions)
- Capital preservation is not conservative — it is the **only** thing that keeps you in the game long enough for your edge to manifest

## Foundation 5 — Trading Psychology
> *YTC Vol 4, Chapter 10*

**Science basis:** The amygdala (threat detection) fires faster than the prefrontal cortex (rational thinking). When money is at risk, your brain is physically working against you.

### Three Psychological Traps

| Trap | Science | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenge trading** | Loss aversion (Kahneman) — losses feel 2× worse than equal gains feel good | Hard session stop rule |
| **Hesitation** | Analysis paralysis under uncertainty | Defined trigger rules — no trigger, no trade |
| **Moving stops** | Hope overrides probability thinking | Pre-commit: stop is placed, cannot be moved against you |

### Peak Performance Mindset

- Focus on **process**, not outcome
- A losing trade that followed your rules = a **good trade**
- A winning trade that broke your rules = a **bad trade**
- Judge yourself only on execution quality, not P&L

## Structured Learning Path: Theory → Practice

### Stage 1 — Establish Foundation (no live money)

| Week | Theory | Practice |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Vol 2: Principles of Markets, how price moves | Read 100 past charts — identify trends and consolidations only |
| 3–4 | Vol 2: Market Structure, S/R, Multiple TF Analysis | Market Structure Journal — annotate charts daily |
| 5–6 | Vol 2: Strength & Weakness, Future Trend Direction | Annotate charts: identify strength and weakness |
| 7–8 | Vol 3: Setup types, Entry, Stop, Target rules | Identify setups on historical charts — no execution yet |
| 9–10 | Vol 4: Money Management, Trading Plan, Procedures | Write your Trading Plan and Procedures Manual |
| 11–12 | Vol 4: Psychology chapters | Begin Motivation Journal + Lessons Learnt Journal |

---

### Stage 2 — Simulator Trading

| Activity | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Trade simulator daily (full session) | Build execution habits without financial consequence |
| Follow Procedures Manual exactly | Identify where your process breaks down |
| Complete Trade Journal after every session | Capture entry reason, stop, target, result, emotion state |
| Weekly replay review | Deliberate practice loop: **Trade → Record → Review → Improve** |

**Target:** 3 consecutive months of **process consistency** before moving to Stage 3.
Not 3 profitable months — process consistency.

---

### Stage 3 — Live, Minimum Size

- Smallest possible position size (1 micro lot, 1 share, 1 contract)
- Identical process to simulator — only difference is real money
- Psychology shifts dramatically here — observe your own reactions without judgment
- Goal: maintain the same decision quality under real financial pressure

---

### Stage 4 — Gradual Scale Up

- Only increase size after documented consistency at current size
- Scale slowly — each size increase triggers psychology recalibration
- Track expectancy across at least **50 trades** before evaluating the system

## Daily Practice Routine

### Pre-Session (30 min before open)

```
[ ] Review higher timeframe charts
[ ] Mark key S/R levels
[ ] Identify market structure
[ ] Formulate directional bias (or "no bias — wait")
[ ] Identify potential setup areas for the session
[ ] Review Trading Plan / Procedures checklist
```

### During Session

```
[ ] Follow Ongoing Market Analysis process — bar by bar
[ ] Evaluate each potential setup against your checklist
[ ] If setup triggers → execute per plan, no improvisation
[ ] If no setup → do nothing  (doing nothing IS a decision)
```

### Post-Session (30–60 min)

```
[ ] Record every trade in Trades Journal
      — setup type, entry, stop, target, result, emotional state
[ ] Record session observations in Trading Log
[ ] Note any deviation from plan — why did it happen?
[ ] Write one key lesson in Lessons Learnt Journal
[ ] Update stats: win rate, average R:R, expectancy
```

### Weekly Review

```
[ ] Replay the week's sessions using market replay tool
[ ] Re-annotate market structure from scratch
      — compare to what you saw in real time
[ ] Identify best trade and worst trade — extract lessons from both
[ ] Update Trading Plan if rules need refinement
```
